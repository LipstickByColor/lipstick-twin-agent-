How twin. works

Same color, very different price tags. We find the lipsticks that look identical on your lips — then an AI agent shops around so you don't have to.

1
💄 You pick a shade
Tell us the lipstick you're obsessed with.
2
🎨 Color science finds its twins
We measure shades in CIELAB, the color space of human eyesight. Any shade closer than ΔE 2.3 looks identical on lips — that's a twin.
ΔE 1.1 — two shades. your eye sees one.
3
🤖 An AI agent goes shopping
For each twin it runs a live Google search, opens real retailer pages, and verifies the brand, shade and price on the page itself. That's why results take a minute or two.
4
🏷️ Cheapest twin wins
Verified in-stock prices get ranked. You see the identical shade for the lowest price — with links to buy it.
What's ΔE? A score for how different two colors look to a human. Below 1: identical. Below 2.3: you can't spot the difference side by side. We only call a shade your twin under that line.
